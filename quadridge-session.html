Live Notes — Context

Six Surgical Questions

Deploy responsively — don't read these in order. Listen for which use case is getting energy and follow it. Click each question when asked.

✓ ASKED
Document Analysis
Q1: "When a new transaction lands — walk me through it. Who receives the 600 pages? What do they do first? What are they actually looking for? How long before the team feels confident they understand the structure?"
Listen for: Named people, specific steps, time estimates, what gets missed. If Sinah does this work, get her version — it'll be more honest than David's.
✓ ASKED
Board Packs
Q2: "On the board packs — how far in advance do you receive them? What's the prep process before a board meeting? Where do the red flags usually hide — in the financials, the narrative, or in what's missing?"
Listen for: Time pressure (days vs weeks), whether they're rubber-stamping or actually reviewing, specific examples of things they've caught or missed.
✓ ASKED
Covenant Monitoring
Q3: "On the covenant monitoring — how does the data come in? Email, portal, spreadsheet? What does the checking process look like today? How often do breaches get caught late?"
Listen for: Data format and delivery method, Excel dependency, frequency of checks, consequences of late catches. This tells us integration complexity.
✓ ASKED
Deadlines & Compliance
Q4: "How are tax deadlines and filing dates currently tracked? Is it a shared system or individual knowledge? What happened last time something slipped?"
Listen for: Whether tracking is centralised or in someone's head, real consequences of missed deadlines (regulatory, financial, reputational), frequency of near-misses.
✓ ASKED
Technology
Q5: "What systems are you running today? Email client, document management, anything for covenant tracking? Has anyone tried any AI tools internally?"
Listen for: Outlook vs Gmail, any DMS, Excel vs specialist tools, cloud vs on-prem, any existing AI usage (even ChatGPT for ad hoc tasks).
✓ ASKED
Critical Anchor
Q6: "If we get this right — what would have to be true for you to say in six months' time, this was one of the best decisions we made this year?"
Listen for: Whether the answer is about cost saving, risk reduction, capacity, or credibility with their bank clients. This sets the success criteria in their language. Write it down verbatim.

Asset Management Case Study — Deep Dive

Jeremy walked through this on the discovery call and it landed. In the whiteboard, go deeper:

The setup: Private credit asset manager. 500-page lending documents. Need to monitor covenants monthly — leverage ratios, cash reserves, reporting obligations. Previously done by humans reading PDFs and entering into spreadsheets.

What we built: AI ingests the lending document, extracts the seven things that need monitoring, tells you exactly which page it found each one on. Dashboard shows 100 companies: these three are amber, this one has breached cash reserve. Source referencing on every flag so the analyst can verify in seconds.

Why it's relevant: This is almost exactly what Quadridge does — structured finance oversight, covenant monitoring, document analysis. Different geography, same problem pattern.

Draw on the whiteboard

The before/after of their document analysis workflow:

Today: 800 pages arrive → human skims → manual extraction → hope nothing was missed → Excel tracking → human checks covenants.

Tomorrow: 800 pages arrive → AI extracts obligations, covenants, key terms → human reviews structured summary with source links → dashboard flags what needs attention → analyst focuses only on exceptions.

Let them correct the diagram. The corrections are the signal we need.

The Close

"Here's what we'll do next. We'll take everything from today and turn it into a pre-proposal within two weeks — a clear problem statement, a proposed solution, a timeline, and a cost. We'll send it over and you tell us where we've got it wrong."

Then the practical: "To make it as specific as possible, it would help to get a couple of sample documents — even heavily redacted. And we should get the NDA signed so both sides can speak freely."

The one next step: Specific action + named owner + specific date. All agreed before the call ends.

"We'll be in touch" is not a next step. Get a date.
